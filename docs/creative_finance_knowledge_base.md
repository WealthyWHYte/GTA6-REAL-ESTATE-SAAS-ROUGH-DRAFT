## Strategy 1: Subject-To (SubTo)

### Definition
Buyer takes title to the property while the existing mortgage remains in the seller's name. You make the payments directly to the lender.

### When to Use
- Seller has an existing mortgage with favorable terms (low rate)
- Seller is behind on payments or facing foreclosure
- Property has equity but seller needs cash quickly
- Seller is motivated but can't qualify for traditional financing

### Key Terms
- **Down Payment:** Typically 0-10% of equity
- **Closing:** 7-21 days
- **Possession:** Immediate
- **Risk:** Junior lien, due on sale clause

### Seller Benefits
- Receives lump sum cash
- Avoids foreclosure
- Gets relieved of mortgage obligation (you pay)
- No showings/open houses

### Buyer Benefits
- Low/no down payment
- Instant equity
- Cash flow from day one
- No qualifying

### Warning Signs
- High mortgage balance vs value
- ARMAdjustable Rate about to adjust
- Seller in active bankruptcy

## Strategy 2: Seller Finance (Installment Sale)

### Definition
Seller acts as the bank. You pay the seller directly in monthly installments over an agreed term (typically 5-30 years).

### When to Use
- Property is free-and-clear (no existing mortgage)
- Seller wants ongoing monthly income
- Seller is motivated but prefers steady cash flow
- Conventional financing unavailable

### Key Terms
- **Down Payment:** 5-20%
- **Interest Rate:** Typically 6-10% (above market)
- **Term:** 5-30 years
- **Balloon:** Often 5-10 years

### Seller Benefits
- Monthly income stream
- Tax benefits (interest)
- Keeps title until paid off
- Higher sale price possible

### Buyer Benefits
- No bank qualifying
- Flexible terms negotiated
- Lower closing costs
- Faster close

### Warning Signs
- Seller needs all cash immediately
- Unrealistic terms

## Strategy 3: Lease Option (Rent-to-Own)

### Definition
Buyer leases the property with an option to purchase at a predetermined price within a specified time frame.

### When to Use
- Buyer needs time to improve credit
- Property needs work before resale
- Seller wants rental income while waiting for better market
- Want to "try before you buy"

### Key Terms
- **Option Fee:** 1-5% of purchase price (non-refundable)
- **Monthly Rent:** Market rate + premium
- **Option Period:** 1-5 years
- **Purchase Price:** Fixed at start (with appreciation credits)

### Seller Benefits
- Ongoing rental income
- Option fee upfront
- Property maintained by tenant-buyer
- Price locked in current market

### Buyer Benefits
- Time to build credit/down payment
- Lock in today's price
- Test property viability
- Minimal initial outlay

### Warning Signs
- Option fee too high
- Rental premium excessive
- No appreciation credit clause

## Strategy 4: Hybrid Subject-To + Seller Finance

### Definition
Combines taking over existing mortgage (SubTo) AND seller financing the equity portion in one transaction.

### When to Use
- Property has existing mortgage AND significant equity
- Seller wants some cash now AND monthly income
- You want to minimize cash out of pocket
- Bridge between SubTo and Seller Finance

### Example Structure:
- **Existing Mortgage:** $500,000 (you assume)
- **Equity:** $300,000 (seller finances)
- **Your Payment:** $3,500/mo (covers both)
- **Seller Payment:** $2,000/mo (equity portion)

### Key Terms
- **Down Payment:** Minimal to none
- **Equity Terms:** 5-15 year amortization
- **Interest:** Market rate + 2-3%

### Warning Signs
- Combined payment exceeds cash flow
- Complex subordination agreements needed
- Seller won't agree to hybrid

## Strategy 5: Wraparound Mortgage

### Definition
A new mortgage "wraps around" the existing mortgage. You collect payments from the buyer that exceed your payment to the original lender.

### When to Use
- Existing mortgage has very low rate
- Significant equity gap
- Seller willing to carry back financing
- Creating owner financing without paying off existing loan

### Key Terms
- **New Loan Amount:** Full property value
- **Existing Loan:** Continues in background
- **Spread:** Your interest rate minus seller's rate
- **Equity:** Difference between value and total debt

### Seller Benefits
- No need to pay off existing loan
- Monthly income stream
- Avoids prepayment penalties

### Buyer Benefits
- Lower payments than conventional
- Favorable terms
- Cash flow positive from day one

### Warning Signs
- Due-on-sale clause risk
- Complex accounting
- Junior lien position

## Strategy 6: Land Contract (Contract for Deed)

### Definition
Buyer makes payments directly to seller. Seller retains legal title until final payment. Buyer gets equitable title and possession.

### When to Use
- Buyer can't qualify for traditional financing
- Seller wants to avoid foreclosure process
- Property has clear title issues
- State allows favorable land contract terms

### Key Terms
- **Down Payment:** 5-20%
- **Term:** 5-30 years
- **Interest:** Negotiated
- **Default:** Seller retains payments made, can evict

### Seller Benefits
- Keeps legal title
- Monthly income
- Faster than foreclosure
- Tax advantages

### Buyer Benefits
- No bank required
- Immediate possession
- Build equity over time
- Cheaper than rent

### Warning Signs
- Strict state laws
- Hard to resell before paid off
- balloon payments

## Strategy 7: Master Lease (Sandwich Lease)

### Definition
You lease a property from the owner, then sublease it to another tenant. You profit from the spread.

### When to Use
- No money for down payment
- Want to test a market area
- Building property management experience
- Control property without ownership

### Key Terms
- **Master Lease Term:** 1-5 years
- **Option to Purchase:** Often included
- **Sublease Rate:** Market rate + premium
- **Profit:** Spread between rents

### Master Lease Owner Benefits
- No management hassle
- Guaranteed rent
- Property maintained
- Option to sell later

### Sub-Tenant Benefits
- Move-in ready property
- Flexible lease terms
- Option to purchase later

### Your Benefits
- Control with no capital
- Cash flow from spread
- Option to buy later
- No foreclosure risk

### Warning Signs
- Phantom rent schemes illegal in many states
- Must follow landlord-tenant laws
- Squatter risks

## Strategy 8: Assignment of Contract (Wholesale)

### Definition
You find a distressed property, get it under contract, then assign that contract to an end buyer for a fee.

### When to Use
- No money for down payment
- Don't want to own property
- Quick cash with minimal risk
- Building buyer list relationships

### Key Terms
- **Assignment Fee:** $5,000-$50,000+
- **Contract Terms:** Must allow assignment
- **Due Diligence:** 7-30 days
- **Close:** Typically 14-30 days

### Benefits
- No money out of pocket (using OPM)
- Quick turnaround (days-weeks)
- No ownership/triple net costs
- Scalable business model

### Process
1. Find motivated seller
2. Negotiate contract at discount
3. Find end buyer (investor)
4. Assign contract for fee
5. Close happens between seller and buyer

### Warning Signs
- Contract doesn't allow assignment
- Seller won't cooperate
- End buyer falls through

---

## Strategy 9: Double Close ( Simultaneous Close)

### Definition
Close on the property with your money, then immediately close on the same property to the end buyer using their financing.

### When to Use
- Assignment not allowed by contract
- End buyer needs you on title briefly
- Building buyer relationships
- Commercial properties

### Key Terms
- **Funding:** Hard money or private money for first close
- **Spread:** Difference between buy and sell price
- **Timeline:** Same day or 1-3 days apart
- **Costs:** Double closing costs

### Benefits
- Higher fees than wholesale
- Legal way to control deal
- End buyer sees you as principal

### Warning Signs
- Hard money falling through
- End buyer financing fails
- Double closing costs
- State regulations

## AI Underwriter Decision Matrix

### Step 1: Check Existing Mortgage
| Scenario | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| Has mortgage + equity | SubTo or Hybrid |
| Free-and-clear | Seller Finance or Land Contract |
| No mortgage data | Check public records |

### Step 2: Check Seller Motivation
| Scenario | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| Long DOM (>90 days) | SubTo, Wholesale |
| Behind on payments | SubTo |

| Equity | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| $0-$100K | Lease Option |
| $100K-$500K | SubTo |
| $500K-$1M | Hybrid, Seller Finance |
| $1M+ | Seller Finance, Land Contract |

### Step 4: Check Available Capital
| Cash Available | Strategy |
|----------------|----------|
| $0 | Assignment, Master Lease |
| $5K-$25K | Lease Option |
| $25K-$100K | SubTo, Land Contract |
| $100K+ | Seller Finance, Double Close |
