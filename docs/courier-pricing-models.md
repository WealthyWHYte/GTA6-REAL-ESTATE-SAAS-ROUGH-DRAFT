# GTA6.COURIER Pricing Models Guide

## Overview

This guide covers the 4 primary pricing models used in courier and last-mile delivery contracts. Understanding each model ensures you price profitably while remaining competitive.

---

## 1️⃣ Per Mile Pricing

### When to Use
- Long-haul routes between cities
- Inter-city deliveries
- Medical courier runs between facilities
- Any contract where distance is the primary variable

### Formula
```
Rate = (Operating Cost per Mile × Distance) + Target Margin
```

### Operating Cost Per Mile Calculator

| Vehicle Type | Gas | Maintenance | Insurance | Depreciation | **Total/Mile** |
|--------------|-----|-------------|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Car | $0.12 | $0.04 | $0.05 | $0.08 | **$0.29** |
| Sprinter Van | $0.18 | $0.06 | $0.08 | $0.12 | **$0.44** |
| Box Truck | $0.28 | $0.10 | $0.12 | $0.18 | **$0.68** |
| Semi | $0.45 | $0.18 | $0.15 | $0.25 | **$1.03** |

### Example: Sprinter - 50 Mile Route
```
Operating Cost: 50 miles × $0.44 = $22.00
Target Margin (20%): $22.00 × 0.20 = $4.40
Your Rate: $26.40 → Round to $27.00 or $30.00
```

### Pros
- Simple to calculate
- Directly tied to cost
- Easy for clients to understand

### Cons
- Doesn't account for time
- Traffic can erode margin
- May underprice in high-traffic areas

## 2️⃣ Flat Fee Pricing

### When to Use
- Fleet replacement contracts
- Routes with variable stops
- Same-day rush delivery
- Daily routed contracts
- Areas with predictable traffic

### Formula
```
Flat Rate = (Average Cost per Route × Risk Factor) + Target Margin
```

### Risk Factors
| Factor | Multiplier | When to Apply |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| Rush/Hot Shot | 1.5x | Same-day, time-critical |
| Weather Risk | 1.25x | Winter, monsoon season |
| Variable Stops | 1.3x | 50-100 stops/day |
| High Value Cargo | 1.4x | Medical, electronics |
| Peak Hours | 1.2x | 7-9am, 4-7pm |

### Example: Daily Medical Route
```
Avg operating cost: $180/day
Risk factor (variable stops): 1.3x
Operating with risk: $180 × 1.3 = $234.00
Target margin (25%): $234 × 0.25 = $58.50
Your Flat Rate: $292.50 → Round to $295/day
```

### Pros
- Predictable revenue
- Protected from traffic delays
- Easier for client budgeting

### Cons
- Underestimate = lost margin
- Requires accurate route analysis
- May be too high for simple routes

## 3️⃣ Per Stop Pricing

### When to Use
- Added stops to existing routes
- Multi-stop delivery runs
- Distribution with many drops
- Retail restocking

### Formula
```
Per Stop Rate = (Vehicle Hourly Cost ÷ Expected Stops/Hour) + Margin
```

### Expected Stops Per Hour Guide

| Environment | Stops/Hour | Notes |
|-------------|------------|-------|
| Urban Dense | 15-20 | Multiple buildings |
| Urban Standard | 12-15 | Standard commercial |
| Suburban | 10-12 | Shopping centers |
| Industrial | 8-10 | Warehouses, factories |

### Example: Urban Medical Supply Delivery
```
Vehicle hourly cost (Sprinter): $28.00/hour
Expected stops: 15/hour
Cost per stop: $28.00 ÷ 15 = $1.87
Add margin (25%): $1.87 × 1.25 = $2.34
Your Per Stop Rate: $2.50/route stop
```

### Pros
- Scales with volume
- Fair for both parties
- Incentivizes efficiency

### Cons
- Low stops = lower revenue
- Time-consuming for drivers
- Requires route optimization

## 4️⃣ Per Piece/Package Pricing

### When to Use
- E-commerce fulfillment
- Medical specimen transport
- Document courier
- Parts delivery
- Pharmacy delivery

### Formula
```
Per Piece Rate = (Avg Handling Cost × Handling Factor) + Margin
```

### Handling Factors
| Package Type | Factor | Reason |
|--------------|--------|--------|
| Documents | 1.0x | Light, easy |
| Small Parcel | 1.2x | Standard handling |
| Large Parcel | 1.5x | Two-person lift |
| Medical Specimen | 2.0x | Chain of custody |
| Temperature Sensitive | 2.5x | Climate control |

### Example: Medical Specimen Transport
```
Base handling cost: $3.50
Medical factor: 2.0x
Cost per piece: $3.50 × 2.0 = $7.00
Margin (30%): $7.00 × 0.30 = $2.10
Your Per Piece Rate: $9.10 → $10.00
```

### Pros
- Directly tied to work done
- Easy for clients to budget
- Scales with volume

### Cons
- Underpricing common
- Doesn't account for distance
- Need to know package mix

## 📊 Pricing Strategy Matrix

### By Vehicle Type

| Vehicle | Best Model | Reason |
|---------|------------|--------|
| Car | Per Mile + Per Piece | Low overhead, package flexibility |
| Sprinter | Flat Fee + Per Stop | Daily routes, medical, high value |
| Box Truck | Flat Fee + Per Mile | Consistent volume, long distances |
| Semi | Per Mile | Long haul, fuel-focused |

### By Industry

| Industry | Recommended Model | Rationale |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Medical | Flat Fee (routed) + Per Piece | Daily schedules, chain of custody |
| Legal | Per Piece + Flat Fee | Documents, court filings |
| IT/Technology | Flat Fee + Per Mile | High value, time-sensitive |
| E-commerce | Per Stop + Per Piece | Variable volume |
| Parts/Auto | Per Mile + Flat Fee | Distance-based, scheduled |

## 🎯 Margin Guidelines

### Minimum Margins by Model

| Model | Minimum Margin | Target Margin |
|-------|----------------|---------------|
| Per Mile | 15% | 20-25% |
| Flat Fee | 20% | 25-30% |
| Per Stop | 20% | 25-30% |
| Per Piece | 25% | 30-40% |

### Why Margins Matter
- Fuel fluctuation
- Vehicle breakdown
- Customer disputes
- Insurance claims
- Idle time between contracts
- Administrative overhead

## 🔥 Pro Tips

1. **Never price below operating cost**
   - Know your exact cost per mile

2. **Build in buffer**
   - Add 10-15% for unexpected costs

3. **Get commitment**
   - Minimum volume guarantees
   - 30-day rolling contracts

4. **Review quarterly**
   - Fuel prices change
   - Insurance rates change
   - Adjust accordingly

5. **Document everything**
   - Use rate sheets
   - Get signatures
   - Clear scope of work
