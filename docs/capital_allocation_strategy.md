# Capital Allocation Strategy
## From Hunter to Allocator

---

## The Core Philosophy

This system is designed for operators who want to:
1. Build capital reserves (assignment/flip focus)
2. Transition to cash-flow portfolio (hold focus)
3. Ultimately become capital allocators

The key is **sequencing**, not choosing one or the other.

---

## Phase 1: Capital Accumulation (Months 1-6)

### Focus: Assignment Velocity
- Send 100+ offers/week
- Target $25K average assignment fee
- Close 2-3 deals/month
- Goal: $150K-250K liquid reserves

### Rules:
- No holds until reserves hit $150K minimum
- Every dollar funds the war chest
- Resist emotional attachment to deals
- If it doesn't assignment cleanly, pass

### Success Metric:
$150K+ liquid reserves = psychological safety achieved

---

## Phase 2: Hybrid Deployment (Months 6-12)

### Focus: Selective Holds + Continued Assignments
- Keep 1 out of every 4 viable hold deals
- 75% assignment focus
- 25% hold focus

### Allocation Formula (Per Deal):
- 40% → Operating capital reserve
- 30% → War chest for next hold
- 30% → Reserved profit/safety

### Rules:
- Only keep deals with $700+/month cash flow
- Must have 20%+ payment advantage vs retail
- Entry capital must be <20% of assignment fee potential
- Must pass all tightened underwriting filters

### Success Metric:
$10K-15K/month passive income + $250K+ reserves

---

## Phase 3: Portfolio Accumulation (Year 2+)

### Focus: Shift to Holds
- 50/50 split or 70/30 hold/assignment
- Assignments fund additional holds
- Passive income becomes primary

### Rules:
- Never hold a deal that only works on appreciation speculation
- Only deploy if 6+ months personal + business runway exists
- Always maintain 6-month reserve buffer

### Success Metric:
$20K+/month passive income

---

## Freedom Threshold Definition

**Freedom = $250K liquid + $12K/month passive + working engine**

Not $100K/month (that's empire mode).

This framework removes the emotional pressure of "needing" massive revenue immediately.

---

## The Dangerous Trap to Avoid

**Pure assignment mode:**
- Resets to zero every month
- Always hunting, never resting
- No compounding

**Pure hold mode early:**
- Creates liquidity problems
- Stresses decision-making
- Delays financial security

**Hybrid mode:**
- Stacks reserves
- Builds passive income
- Maintains optionality

---

## Decision Matrix: Keep or Assign?

Use this filter:

| Factor | Keep (Hold) | Assign |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Cash Flow | $700+/mo | <$700/mo |
| Payment Advantage | >20% | <15% |
| Entry Capital | <20% of assignment fee potential | N/A |
| Runway | 6+ months exists | N/A |
| Debt Structure | Elite (<5% or Sub-To) | Standard |

**Rule:** If 3+ columns say "Keep" → Keep. Otherwise → Assign.

## Acquisition Fee Strategy

When structuring holds, always consider:

### Option A: Standard Hold
- Buy property directly
- No upfront fee to self

### Option B: Entity Structuring
- Form LLC/entity to buy
- Invoice acquisition fee (5-10% of purchase price)
- Keeps capital in your pocket while owning asset
- Common in investor communities

### Example:
$500K property → $25K-50K acquisition fee
+ $800/month cash flow
+ Equity buildup
+ Tax benefits

This creates "upfront + ongoing" value, reducing the need to choose between cash and cash flow.

---

## Emergency Protocol

**If $0 assignments for 60 days:**
- Do NOT panic-sell holds
- Do NOT take bad assignment deals
- Reduce burn rate
- Focus on offer volume
- Trust the system

**Runway Calculation:**
Personal expenses + Business costs + Marketing = Monthly Burn
Keep 6 months burn in reserves at all times
