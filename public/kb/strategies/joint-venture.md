# Joint Venture Strategy

## Overview
Partner with another investor who provides capital in exchange for equity share. Both parties share profits and losses based on agreed terms.

## When to Use
- Limited personal capital
- Larger deals desired
- Risk diversification
- Need expertise not possessed
- Scaling business quickly

## Structure Options
1. **Equity Split**: Capital vs. effort split
2. **Preferred Return**: Investor gets paid first
3. **50/50 Split**: Equal partnership
4. **Promote Structure**: Performance bonuses

## Key Terms
- **Capital Contribution**: Cash invested
- **Equity Split**: Percentage ownership
- **Preferred Return**: 8-10% (paid first)
- **Promote**: Bonus for hitting targets
- **Management Fee**: 2-5% of deal value

## Pros
- ✅ Access to larger deals
- ✅ Share risk and liability
- ✅ Leverage expertise
- ✅ Scale faster
- ✅ Learn from partners

## Cons
- ❌ Diluted profits
- ❌ Shared control
- ❌ Potential conflicts
- ❌ Complex agreements
- ❌ Dependency on partner

## Typical JV Structure
- Deal: $1,000,000 purchase
- Investor: $500,000 (50%)
- You: $500,000 + expertise
- Split: 50/50 after preferred return
- Preferred Return: 10%

## Finding JV Partners
1. Real estate investment clubs
2. Private investors
3. Hard money lenders
4. Family offices
5. Online investor forums

## Exit Strategies
1. Property sale (split profits)
2. Refinance (cash out)
3. Bring in new partner
4. Buy out partner
