# Lease Option Strategy

## Overview
Lease the property with an option to purchase it at a predetermined price within a specified time period.

## When to Use
- Buyers with poor credit
- Want to "try out" the property
- Need time to qualify for financing
- Future appreciation plays
- Seller willing to carry terms

## Structure
- **Option Fee**: Non-refundable fee for right to buy
- **Monthly Rent**: Includes rent credit toward purchase
- **Purchase Price**: Fixed at contract time
- **Option Period**: 12-36 months typical

## Key Terms
- **Option Fee**: $5,000-$25,000 (non-refundable)
- **Rent Credit**: 10-50% of monthly rent
- **Purchase Price**: Fixed or formula-based
- **Term**: 12-36 months

## Pros
- ✅ Low upfront ($5-25K)
- ✅ Test market before buying
- ✅ Lock in current price
- ✅ No forced appreciation
- ✅ Build equity with rent credits

## Cons
- ❌ Option may expire
- ❌ Maintenance responsibility
- ❌ Complex tax implications
- ❌ No ownership until exercise

## Typical Deal
- Option Fee: $10,000
- Monthly Rent: $3,000
- Rent Credit: $1,000/month (toward purchase)
- Purchase Price: $500,000
- Option Term: 24 months
- Interest Rate: 7% (if financing)

## Exit Strategies
1. Exercise option before expiration
2. Assign option to another buyer
3. Let option expire (walk away)
4. Sell lease option to investor
