# Seller Financing Strategy

## Overview
The seller acts as the lender, financing all or part of the purchase price. You make payments directly to the seller.

## When to Use
- Sellers with existing mortgages (subject to existing financing)
- Seller wants ongoing income
- Poor buyer credit
- Traditional financing unavailable
- Seller willing to carry note

## Structure Options
1. **Full Seller Financing**: Seller carries entire purchase price
2. **Partial Financing**: Seller carries second mortgage
3. **Junior Lien**: Seller subordinate to existing mortgage

## Key Terms
- **Down Payment**: 5-30%
- **Interest Rate**: 6-10% (negotiable)
- **Term**: 1-10 years (balloon)
- **Monthly Payment**: P&I or interest-only

## Pros
- ✅ Lower down payment than traditional
- ✅ Flexible terms
- ✅ Build equity over time
- ✅ Interest may be below market
- ✅ No bank qualifying

## Cons
- ❌ Interest rate may be high
- ❌ Due on sale clause (seller could call loan)
- ❌ Credit requirements vary
- ❌ Balloon payment

## Typical Deal Structure
- Purchase Price: $500,000
- Down Payment: $50,000 (10%)
- Seller Carry: $450,000
- Interest Rate: 7%
- Term: 5 years (balloon)
- Monthly Payment: ~$3,000

## Exit Strategies
1. Refinance with traditional lender
2. Sell to end buyer
3. Cash-out refinance
4. Pay off over time
