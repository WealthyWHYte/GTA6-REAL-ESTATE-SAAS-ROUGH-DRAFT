# Subject To Strategy

## Overview
Take over the existing mortgage payments without paying off the loan. You acquire the property "subject to" the existing financing.

## When to Use
- Sellers with low interest rate mortgages (3-6%)
- Distressed properties
- Sellers behind on payments
- Properties with significant equity
- Quick equity plays

## Key Terms
- **Down Payment**: Often $0-$10,000
- **Existing Loan**: Assumes payments
- **Due On Sale Clause**: Risk of acceleration
- **Transfer**: May need seller cooperation

## Pros
- ✅ Little to no money down
- ✅ Take over low interest rate
- ✅ Instant equity
- ✅ No qualifying
- ✅ Cash flow positive immediately

## Cons
- ❌ Due on sale clause (loan could be called)
- ❌ Credit liability (your credit at risk)
- ❌ Legal complexity
- ❌ Seller default risk
- ❌ Insurance issues

## Risk Mitigation
1. Keep payments current
2. Maintain escrow for taxes/insurance
3. Limited recourse states
4. Inter Vivos trusts
5. Installment land contracts

## Typical Deal
- Purchase Price: $400,000
- Existing Loan: $280,000 (4% rate)
- Equity: $120,000
- Down Payment: $5,000
- Monthly Payment: $1,800 (assumed)

## Exit Strategies
1. Refinance (when qualified)
2. Sell to end buyer
3. Cash-out refi
4. Seller finance secondary
