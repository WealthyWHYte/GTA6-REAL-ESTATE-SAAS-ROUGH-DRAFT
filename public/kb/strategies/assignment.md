# Assignment / Wholesale Strategy

## Overview
Assign your purchase contract to an end buyer for an assignment fee. You never actually buy the property - you transfer the contract rights.

## When to Use
- Limited capital
- Want quick returns without owning property
- Strong buyer network
- Volume-based business model
- Properties under market value

## How It Works
1. Get property under contract at low price
2. Find end buyer willing to pay more
3. Assign contract for fee
4. Close with end buyer

## Key Terms
- **Assignment Fee**: $5,000-$50,000+
- **Contract Must Allow**: Assignment
- **Due Diligence**: Complete before assigning
- **Closing**: End buyer closes directly

## Pros
- ✅ Low capital required
- ✅ Fast returns (days to weeks)
- ✅ No ownership risk
- ✅ Volume scales quickly
- ✅ Lower liability

## Cons
- ❌ Need strong buyer list
- ❌ Limited control
- ❌ Contract restrictions
- ❌ Reputation risk

## Typical Deal
- Purchase Price: $350,000
- ARV: $450,000
- Assignment Fee: $25,000
- End Buyer Pays: $375,000

## Exit Strategies
1. Assign to cash buyer
2. Assign to finance buyer
3. Double close
4. Purchase yourself
