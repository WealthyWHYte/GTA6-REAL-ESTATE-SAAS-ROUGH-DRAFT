# Wrap Around Mortgage Strategy

## Overview
A "wrap" mortgage that wraps around an existing mortgage. The buyer makes payments to the seller, who continues paying the underlying mortgage.

## When to Use
- Seller has low interest rate
- Large gap between value and loan
- Traditional financing unavailable
- Seller willing to carry terms
- Sub2 situations

## Structure
- **Existing Loan**: $200,000 @ 4%
- **New Loan**: $400,000 @ 7%
- **Wrap Amount**: $400,000 (seller keeps difference)

## Key Terms
- **Wrap Note**: New mortgage wraps old
- **Interest Spread**: Seller profits on spread
- **Back-end Loan**: Must be assumable
- **Due on Sale**: Risk exists

## Pros
- ✅ Seller maintains low rate
- ✅ Lower payments
- ✅ Points may be avoided
- ✅ Can build equity
- ✅ Flexible terms

## Cons
- ❌ Due on sale clause
- ❌ Complex legal
- ❌ Credit liability
- ❌ Insurance issues
- ❌ Hard to find

## Typical Deal
- Purchase Price: $500,000
- Existing Loan: $250,000 (3.5%)
- Wrap Loan: $500,000 @ 7%
- Seller Payment: $3,300/mo
- Underlying Payment: $1,200/mo
- Seller Profit: $2,100/mo

## Exit Strategies
1. Refinance when qualified
2. Sell to end buyer
3. Pay off underlying
4. Cash-out refi
